- LINE OF INTERACTION (client / staff contact) -
Front Stage Staff actions visible to client
Receptionist greets client Described in sessions as inconsistent. No standard welcome. No warmth protocol. The problem is motivation and expectation-setting, not staff capability.
Receptionist registers in Salesforce Three entry paths. Registration layer launched March 3. Working as designed - but adds friction at already overloaded front desk.
Presents consent form No script for explaining why. "Please sign here" only. No language for pushback. English only.
No hospitality during wait Receptionist does not offer water, check in on client, or provide wait-time updates. Not trained to. Not expected to.
Routes to program staff if IC unavailable Happens daily before 9:30. No scripted explanation for client. No Salesforce record created for bypass. Sustainability Risk
IC conducts intake assessment Winnie reads from script - not yet conversational. Takes longer than designed. Avg 10.6 min (down from ~20); daily variance 20-150 min total - process not yet stable. Sustainability Risk
Open lobby: two problems, two timelines Immediate: Two chairs + defined corner available now - no budget required. Addresses privacy discomfort without waiting for construction. Permanent: Architectural booth (behind Joe brother mural) - design meeting scheduled, no completion date. Do not let the hard fix block the easy one.
Notepad workaround: design failure, not bright spot IC invented a fix: clients write sensitive answers (income, household) on a notepad rather than saying them aloud. It works. But it requires manual Salesforce entry after the fact - a data integrity risk. And it confirms the designed process is insufficient for a privacy-conscious, 72%-LEP (Limited English Proficiency) population. Needs to become a written protocol. If IC leaves, this fix disappears.
No objection-handling language When client resists or questions the process, IC has no scripted response. Improvises or loses the client.
IC explains eligibility Script exists. "No dead ends" framing is good. Warm in tone when followed.
Eligibility notification ≠ enrollment conversion Pilot observation: clients told they qualify for additional programs respond with indifference - "okay, cool, maybe next time." Consistent with CASL's own SDOH (Social Determinants of Health) auto-referral history, where eligibility notification also failed to drive enrollment. The script needs a warm referral layer: connect eligibility to something the client said during intake. "You mentioned your utility bill - LIHEAP (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program) covers that directly" lands differently than "you also qualify for energy assistance." Eligibility is information. A connection is Coordination. Sustainability Risk
No script for ineligible client What to say when the answer is no - completely absent. Clients leave without an alternative path. Sustainability Risk
IC coordinates handoff Script says "I'll walk you over." In practice: client often told to wait again or given a name without an escort.
Program staff opens session Script: "I've reviewed your intake." Strong language - but only if they actually read it.
Cross-program referral is manual "Persuade client to meet IC for assessment" - per ops gaps matrix. Relies on staff initiative, not system.
No standard follow-through protocol - confirmed by pilot data Six weeks of pilot data: 210 initial visits, 1 follow-up visit. This is not an inferred gap - it is a measured failure visible in the dashboard. Script says "I will follow up by ___." No system enforcement. No Salesforce field to log whether follow-up happened. 48-hour post-appointment SMS exists as infrastructure - this is a configuration decision, not a development project. Move from Phase 2 to immediate. Sustainability Risk

Back Stage Staff actions not visible to client
Peak arrival 9 AM. 10+ clients simultaneously. 1 receptionist. Phone, buzzer, walk-ins, workshop attendees all at once.
Salesforce contact created Central record begins. Three entry paths. Designed to prevent duplicate records.
Consent stored in Salesforce Digital record. No physical signature required. Available to all authorized staff.
IC bypass: informal workaround - 75% rate Pilot data (Mar 3-Apr 9): 124 of 166 client records created by program staff, not IC or reception - a 75% bypass rate. These clients received their presenting service but were not assessed for additional eligibility. Cross-enrollment opportunity missed at point of highest client engagement. Root cause unconfirmed - four hypotheses: (1) privacy discomfort in open lobby; (2) upstream routing failure by reception; (3) program staff workaround behavior; (4) IC unavailability before 9:30 AM. Mo and the reception team have not been formally asked. Do not prescribe a single fix until root cause is confirmed. Sustainability Risk
Intake record built in Salesforce 22 questions. Household, income, employment, health, goals. Feeds eligibility logic.
Refusals tracked via Teams chat Daily totals entered manually. No Salesforce record. No data integrity. Invisible to program staff. Sustainability Risk
Salesforce eligibility logic runs Auto-calculates eligible programs from intake data. Ibrahim's build. Working correctly.
Editing intake = duplicate appt If IC edits intake record, system creates duplicate appointment. Phase 2 fix needed.
Salesforce appt created for program staff IC schedules via Salesforce. Staff notified of ownership. Notification bug resolved.
Program staff may not read record In the pilot, program staff skipping the intake record before a first appointment was the most commonly observed failure point. No accountability mechanism exists - no required field, no check, no tracking. Sustainability Risk
One counselor handles all related services Design principle: single counselor owns all benefit services for client. Working correctly.
Internal referral via Salesforce (when used) Ops gap matrix: "if client has no time, create internal referral through Salesforce." But client must be persuaded first. No automatic connection.
Referrals sitting in queue without action Observed in pilot: cross-program referrals created in Salesforce are not being actioned within 24 hours. The record exists; the follow-through does not. No metric tracks time-from-referral-to-contact. No owner named. This is the Commitment C gap expressed as operational data. Sustainability Risk
Enrollment causation field: exists, not activated A field in the Salesforce program enrollment record already captures whether an enrollment resulted from universal intake. Benefits team does not populate it consistently. Other programs don't know it exists. Activating this as a required field is the only mechanism that proves intake caused cross-enrollment - the primary outcome the pilot was designed to produce. Zero cost. Requires one training conversation + Ibrahim to set as mandatory.
SMS doc reminder sent Automated. Sent after appointment booked. English only currently. Chinese + Spanish: Phase 2.
No day-before or 1-hour reminder Phase 2 enhancement planned. Currently: just the required docs reminder.

ONE CASL Gaps & Opportunities What needs to change
Hospitality moment missing A warm, scripted welcome costs nothing. "We're glad you're here" + explain what will happen next. Not in any current script.
Value proposition missing Client needs to understand: "This registration means you won't have to repeat your story." That sentence is not said.
Consent explanation gap "Sign here" is not enough. Need: why this form exists, what it does for the client, and what to say if they push back.
No Staff Contact During Lobby Wait Lobby wait is an experience. Currently: nothing. Opportunity: branded materials, what to expect signage, warm check-in.
No objection-handling layer Scripts cover ideal path only. Every emotional state - resistant, anxious, in a hurry, lonely - needs specific language.
Ineligible client has no path "Not eligible" should trigger an alternative: community resource, partner referral, return path. Currently nothing. Sustainability Risk
Handoff quality is unmeasured "I'll walk you over" exists in the script. Whether it happens: unknown. Program staff reading intake: unknown. Needs tracking.
Whether staff read the intake record: not tracked The handoff from IC to program staff is where intake data most often gets skipped, and there is no measurement to confirm whether it happens. A simple Salesforce checkbox "intake reviewed" would cost nothing to build. Sustainability Risk
"One start, many paths" not yet real The ONE CASL promise depends on cross-program connection being automatic, not persuasive. Architecture not built yet.
Commitment C is unmet at close "Someone will contact you" in the script. Reality: no enforcement. No tracking. Clients who weren't followed up likely don't return. Sustainability Risk
